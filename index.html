NEPAL

"Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia, bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west. Known for its diverse geography, Nepal ranges from the towering Himalayan mountains, including Mount Everest (the world's highest peak), to the flat Terai plains. Its capital and largest city is Kathmandu."

Key Attractions

Boudhanath

"One of the largest Buddhist stupas in the world, Boudhanath is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its massive white dome and all-seeing eyes symbolize peace and wisdom."

Hanuman Dhoka

"A historic palace complex located in Kathmandu Durbar Square, it once served as the royal residence. Named after the Hindu deity Hanuman."

Garden of Dreams

"A serene neo-classical garden in the heart of Kathmandu, known for its beautiful pavilions, fountains, and lush greenery."

Asan Tole

"A bustling market square, Asan is famous for its vibrant local trade, offering everything from spices to traditional Nepali goods."

Kathmandu Durbar Square

"A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historic square is filled with ancient temples, palaces, and courtyards."

Kumari Bahal(Kumari Ghar)

"A traditional Newari palace, home to the Kumari, the living goddess of Nepal. Known for its exquisite wooden carvings."

Taleju Temple

"Dedicated to the goddess Taleju Bhawani, this temple is one of the tallest and most revered in Kathmandu Durbar Square."

Golden Temple

"A Buddhist monastery in Patan with intricate golden details and a peaceful courtyard. A unique fusion of elements."

Krishna Mandir

"A stunning stone temple in Patan Durbar Square, dedicated to Lord Krishna, known for its intricate carvings."

Patan Durbar Square

"A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its Newari architecture, royal palaces, and artistic masterpieces."

Nyatapola

"Nepal’s tallest pagoda, located in Bhaktapur Durbar Square. Dedicated to Siddhi Lakshmi, it’s known for its symmetry."

Lookout Tower

"A popular spot in Nagarkot, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas, including Mount Everest on clear days."

Changu Narayan

"The oldest temple in Nepal, located on a hilltop near Bhaktapur. A UNESCO World Heritage Site dedicated to Lord Vishnu."
